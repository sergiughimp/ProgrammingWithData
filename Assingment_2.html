Introduction

The Travel Data 2023 dataset offers a comprehensive view of traveler habits, encompassing details about travel origins, destinations, durations, demographics, and expenditures. In this project, I refined the dataset by addressing missing and ambiguous values in critical columns like sex, nights, and expenditures. I also introduced a new metric, expenditure per night, and filtered the data to focus on entries where spending per night was $120 or less. After these adjustments, the dataset consisted of 32,856 rows and 14 columns, providing an insightful overview of travel behaviors and spending patterns. This refined dataset can be leveraged to identify trends and create actionable visualizations.

Methods

The data preparation process included several key steps to ensure the dataset was ready for analysis. First, I cleaned the data by addressing missing or incorrect entries, particularly in the columns for sex, nights, and expenditure. For example, '#NULL!' values were replaced with appropriate substitutes ('Unknown' for sex and 0 for expend and nights), and rows with "Unknown" or "Don't Know/NA" in the sex column were excluded. I also converted the expend and nights columns to float32 for consistency and ease of calculation. Subsequently, I introduced a new metric, expend_per_night, by dividing total expenditure by the number of nights. This metric provided a more accurate view of traveler spending habits. I then filtered the dataset to include only rows where expend_per_night was less than or equal to $120, focusing the analysis on more typical spending behaviors. After these transformations, the dataset included 32,856 rows and 14 columns, with relevant attributes like year, quarter, country, mode, purpose, age, sex, duration, and the new expend_per_night metric. To gain insights into spending patterns, I applied descriptive statistics to the expend_per_night column and visualized the distribution of spending using boxplots and histograms. These visualizations helped to identify any outliers or trends, and grouping the data by categories such as purpose, country, and age allowed me to uncover spending patterns across different traveler segments.

Results

The analysis revealed distinct spending patterns among travelers, particularly when focusing on those who spent $120 or less per night. This filtering process allowed us to isolate more typical spending behaviors, minimizing the impact of extreme outliers. Descriptive statistics, along with visualizations like boxplots and histograms, showcased the distribution of spending and highlighted notable trends or anomalies. Grouping the data by categories like purpose, country, and age provided deeper insights into how spending varied among different traveler demographics. These findings offer valuable information that can guide businesses and policymakers in developing targeted strategies to engage specific traveler segments and make more informed decisions.

Conclusion

The analysis of the Travel Data 2023 dataset has provided valuable insights into traveler spending behaviors, emphasizing typical expenditures per night. By cleaning and refining the dataset, we were able to identify meaningful trends across different traveler demographics, including age, purpose, and country. However, the dataset has limitations, including missing values and potential biases, which may affect the accuracy of the results. Ethical considerations, such as data privacy and the exclusion of ambiguous entries, were also taken into account. The insights from this analysis can assist businesses and policymakers in making data-driven decisions, supporting inclusivity and sustainability within the travel industry.
